EXECUTIVE SUMMARY



Gray Capital LLC has verbally been awarded the acquisition of Fairmont Apartments, a newly
delivered 219-unit Class A multifamily community located in Westerville, Ohio — at the
western edge of New Albany, one of the most affluent and supply-constrained suburbs in the
Columbus MSA. Completed in March 2025 by Preferred Living, Fairmont achieved 96%
occupancy in just 11 months, averaging 19 signed leases per month and demonstrating
exceptional renter demand in a market that ranks #1 in the United States for rent growth
resilience. The asset offers best-in-class construction, a premium amenity package, and the
benefit of a 15-year, 100% tax abatement on improved value running through 2041 — a
structural cash flow advantage that meaningfully accelerates investor returns throughout
the hold period and represents significant embedded value at exit.

The acquisition is being financed with a $43.75M Fannie Mae loan through Greystone at 5.17%
fixed with 48 months of interest-only. Gray Capital will self-manage the asset, retaining full
operational control and capturing management margin. The business plan targets a 7-year
hold with 4% annual rent growth, 93.5%+ economic occupancy, and a 5.50% exit cap rate —
projecting an approximate 13.9% LP IRR, 2.21x equity multiple, and ~8.4% average annual
cash-on-cash return. The equity raise is $22.1M, structured with an 8% cumulative preferred
return and an 80/20 LP/GP split.

INVESTMENT HIGHLIGHTS



Irreplaceable Submarket
  Located on the west edge of New Albany — ranked #25 Best City in the U.S. — with a 3-mile
  average household income of $117,664. 47% of households earn over $100K annually. The
  monthly cost to rent at Fairmont is 63% less than owning a comparable home, anchoring a
  durable rent-by-choice demand base.

Best-in-Market Product
  2025 construction by Preferred Living features two all-season heated pools, a TrackMan golf
  simulator, dual Starbucks coffee bars, a private screening room, 24-hour fitness centers, and co-
  working spaces — differentiating the asset meaningfully from the 2018–2022 comp set trading
  above $1,900/unit.
15-Year Tax Abatement
  Fairmont benefits from a 100% abatement on improved value through 2041, generating
  approximately $900,000+ in annual tax savings. The residential condo plat structure provides
  an additional 37% savings versus commercial millage rates. At the projected 2033 exit,
  approximately 8 years of abatement remain — representing an estimated $7–8M NPV to a
  future buyer, a material pricing tailwind embedded in the underwriting.

Proven Renter Demand
  Fairmont reached 96% leased in just 11 months, averaging 19 leases per month from March
  through January 2026. The T-1 annualized income run rate is approximately $4.34M,
  accelerating from a T-6 run rate of $3.79M — reflecting rapidly stabilizing occupancy and rent
  levels.

Columbus #1 Rent Market
  Columbus is the only major U.S. market to avoid negative lease trade-outs post-COVID while
  absorbing outsized new supply — driven by Intel's $28B+ semiconductor investment, Amazon,
  Meta, and Anduril's Arsenal-1 defense manufacturing campus. The Columbus MSA population is
  projected to grow from 2.2M today to 3.15M by 2050, with an estimated structural housing
  deficit of ~6,000 units per year.

Gray Capital Self-Manages
  Gray Capital retains full operational control of Fairmont, capturing the management margin
  directly rather than paying a third-party fee. This structure enables tighter execution on
  occupancy, ancillary income optimization, and expense management — and aligns Gray
  Capital's interests directly with those of LP partners.
