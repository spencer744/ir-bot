## 3. INVESTMENT THESIS & PHILOSOPHY

### The "Midwest Renaissance" Thesis

Gray Capital takes a contrarian approach to multifamily investing. While many institutional investors focus on Sun Belt and coastal gateway markets, Gray Capital has built its strategy around the conviction that growing secondary and tertiary Midwest markets offer superior risk-adjusted returns. The thesis centers on several structural advantages:

- **Lower cost of living and business-friendly environments** attract population and job growth
- **Limited new supply** relative to Sun Belt markets means less downward pressure on rents and occupancy
- **Higher cap rates and stronger cash flow** compared to coastal markets at acquisition
- **Resilience during downturns** — Midwest markets historically experience less volatility
- **Overlooked by institutional capital** — creating pricing inefficiencies Gray Capital can exploit

Gray Capital targets markets that are "Sun Belt-esque" in their growth trajectories — places like Indianapolis, Columbus (OH), and other metros experiencing above-average population and job growth within fundamentally stable economic environments.

### Conservative Underwriting Philosophy: Actuals Over Proformas

A defining characteristic of Gray Capital's approach is its emphasis on **actual operating performance over projected proforma assumptions**. Key principles include:

- **Underwrite to current actuals**, not speculative rent growth or occupancy projections
- **Conservative rent growth assumptions** — never rely on aggressive proforma rent bumps to make a deal work
- **Stress testing** every deal against adverse scenarios (rate increases, occupancy drops, expense inflation)
- **Cash flow at acquisition** — every deal must produce meaningful cash flow from day one, not just at stabilization
- **Fixed-rate, long-term financing** preferred to mitigate interest rate risk
- **Significant operating reserves** to avoid capital calls — the firm obsesses over buying and financing correctly from the outset

This philosophy is what allowed Gray Capital to navigate 2022–2024 market turbulence without capital calls or investor losses, while many peers faced distress.

### Distress & Opportunity Awareness

Gray Capital has emerged as a recognized thought leader on multifamily debt maturity dynamics and distress opportunities. The firm has published widely-cited research (featured in Bisnow, The Real Deal, and Multifamily Dive) tracking the "wall of maturities" in CMBS multifamily loans. Spencer Gray has been quoted extensively on how extend-and-pretend strategies are reaching their limits, and how this creates acquisition opportunities for well-capitalized, disciplined buyers.

The firm's current positioning is opportunistic — targeting high-quality assets available at discounted valuations from sellers facing maturity pressure, while maintaining strict discipline on entry pricing and financing structure.

## 4. INVESTMENT STRATEGIES

### Value-Add
- Acquiring cash-flowing, typically older assets that may require renovations, upgrades, or operational improvements
- Risk Profile: Low–Moderate
- Target IRR: 15%+
- Target Cash-on-Cash: 8%

### Core Plus
- Acquiring stabilized, newer construction assets with high intrinsic value, requiring minimal physical improvement, located in key or developing areas
- Risk Profile: Low
- Target IRR: 13%+
- Target Cash-on-Cash: 7%

### Build to Rent
- Amenity-rich communities featuring single-family homes, townhomes, duplexes, and condos, managed from a central onsite location
- Risk Profile: Low–Moderate
- Target IRR: 14%+
- Target Cash-on-Cash: 8%

### Opportunistic
- Strategies involving more complexity — ground-up development, lease-up, heavy asset repositioning
- Risk Profile: Moderate–High
- Target IRR: 17%+
- Target Cash-on-Cash: 9%
