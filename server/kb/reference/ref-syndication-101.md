# Real Estate Syndication 101: A Complete Beginner's Guide

## What Is a Real Estate Syndication?

A real estate syndication is a partnership in which a group of investors pool capital to acquire a property too large for any single investor to purchase alone. One party (the sponsor) finds, finances, and manages the deal, while the other parties (limited partners) provide the majority of the equity capital and receive passive economic benefits.

## The Two Key Roles

### General Partner (GP) / Sponsor
The GP is the active operator responsible for sourcing the deal, arranging financing, raising capital from investors, executing the business plan (renovations, management, operations), managing investor relations and reporting, and deciding when to exit. The GP typically co-invests 5-15% of the total equity and earns fees plus a profit share (the "promote").

### Limited Partner (LP) / Passive Investor
LPs provide equity capital and receive ownership benefits without active involvement. As an LP, you invest a defined amount (typically $50K-$100K minimum), receive periodic cash distributions, benefit from depreciation tax deductions via the K-1, share in profits at sale, and have liability limited to your investment amount.

## How Syndications Are Structured

Most syndications use a **Limited Liability Company (LLC)** to hold the property. The operating agreement defines capital contributions, profit allocation, and distribution mechanics.

**The Capital Stack:** Senior debt (mortgage, 60-75% of purchase price), LP equity (80-95% of total equity), and GP equity (5-20% of total equity).

**The Distribution Waterfall:** Cash flows are distributed in tiers: (1) return of capital to LPs, (2) preferred return to LPs (commonly 6-10% annualized), (3) LP/GP profit split (e.g., 70/30), and (4) promote tiers that reward the GP for exceeding return thresholds.

## Why People Invest in Syndications

1. **Cash flow** --- Quarterly or monthly distributions from rental income
2. **Appreciation** --- Value growth realized at sale through renovations and operational improvements
3. **Tax benefits** --- Depreciation deductions can reduce or defer taxes on investment income
4. **Diversification** --- Low correlation with stocks and bonds, plus inflation hedging
5. **Passive income** --- Ownership benefits without landlord responsibilities

## How Syndications Differ from Alternatives

| Feature | Syndication | REIT | Crowdfunding | Direct Ownership |
|---------|------------|------|--------------|-----------------|
| Control | None (GP manages) | None | None | Full |
| Minimum | $50K-$100K | As low as $1 | $500-$5K | Full price |
| Liquidity | Illiquid (3-7 yrs) | Public REITs liquid | Limited | Illiquid |
| Tax Benefits | Full pass-through K-1 | Dividends taxed as income | Varies | Full |
| Accreditation | Usually required | Not required (public) | Varies | Not required |

## Types of Strategies

- **Core/Core-Plus:** Lower risk, stabilized properties, 6-10% annual returns
- **Value-Add:** Moderate risk, below-market properties improved via renovations and management, 13-20% IRR target
- **Opportunistic/Development:** Higher risk, ground-up or major repositioning, 18%+ IRR target

## Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **General Partner (GP)** | The sponsor/operator who manages the investment |
| **Limited Partner (LP)** | A passive investor who contributes capital |
| **IRR** | Internal Rate of Return --- annualized return accounting for cash flow timing |
| **Equity Multiple** | Total cash received divided by total cash invested (e.g., 2.0x = doubled your money) |
| **Cash-on-Cash** | Annual cash distributions divided by initial capital invested |
| **Preferred Return** | Minimum annualized return LPs receive before the GP earns any promote |
| **Promote** | The GP's profit share above the preferred return |
| **Waterfall** | Tiered distribution structure defining the order and split of cash flows |
| **Capital Call** | A request for additional capital from LPs beyond the initial investment |
| **K-1** | IRS tax form reporting each partner's share of income, deductions, and credits |
| **Cost Segregation** | Engineering study that accelerates depreciation into shorter recovery periods |
| **Cap Rate** | Net Operating Income divided by property value; a yield metric for valuation |
| **NOI** | Net Operating Income --- revenue minus operating expenses, excluding debt service |
| **LTV** | Loan-to-Value ratio --- mortgage balance divided by appraised value |
| **Basis Points** | One hundredth of a percentage point (100 bps = 1%) |
| **Accredited Investor** | SEC-defined status based on income/net worth thresholds for private offerings |
| **PPM** | Private Placement Memorandum --- the legal disclosure document for the offering |
| **Subscription Agreement** | Legal contract committing capital and confirming investor eligibility |
| **Depreciation** | Non-cash tax deduction for theoretical decline in building value (27.5 years residential) |
| **1031 Exchange** | IRS provision deferring capital gains taxes by reinvesting into like-kind property |
