## What the Market Pays for Abatement

The $12.2M NPV abatement creates a structural pricing advantage that institutional buyers consistently pay for at exit. At Fairmont's projected June 2033 exit:
- ~8 years of 100% abatement remain
- Remaining NPV to buyer: estimated $7–8M
- This represents a price floor under our 5.50% exit cap — no unabated comp can compete on yield when the abatement advantage is adjusted in

Institutional buyers underwriting net-net economics will price the abatement benefit into their acquisition cap rate. A buyer acquiring at 5.50% without abatement vs. 5.50% with $7–8M of remaining abatement NPV is getting a fundamentally different deal. This supports cap rate compression vs. unabated comps at exit.

## Why Fairmont Wins vs. Comps at Exit

At our projected 2033 exit, prospective buyers will compare Fairmont against the then-current comp set. Fairmont's advantages at that time:
1. **Still relatively new construction** (8 years old, vs. 14–18 years for today's 2018–2020 comps)
2. **8 years of abatement remaining** — something no 2018–2020 comp will carry
3. **Best-in-submarket amenity package** — unless something better is built (supply constraint limits this)
4. **Proven occupancy track record** — stabilized in 11 months; rental demand demonstrated
5. **Intel Ohio One timing** — 3,000+ engineers at $135K+ may be arriving exactly as we exit

These factors support our 5.50% exit cap underwriting and provide meaningful cushion against cap rate widening.
