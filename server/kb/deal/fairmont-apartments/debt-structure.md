# Fairmont Apartments — Debt Structure

## Loan Overview

Fairmont is financed with a **Fannie Mae agency loan originated through Greystone**, one of the nation's leading agency lenders. Agency financing is the gold standard for stabilized multifamily — fixed rates, no floating rate exposure, no bridge loan maturity risk.

| Loan Detail | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| **Loan Amount** | $43,750,000 |
| **Lender** | Fannie Mae via Greystone |
| **Interest Rate** | 5.17% fixed |
| **Loan Term** | 7 years |
| **Interest-Only Period** | 48 months (4 years) |
| **LTV** | 70.0% |
| **Going-In DSCR** | 1.03x |
| **Rate Buydown** | $875,000 (2.0% of loan amount) |
| **Amortization** | 30-year schedule (after IO period) |

## Why Agency Financing Matters

**Fannie Mae agency debt is the safest debt structure for a multifamily hold.** Here's why it matters for investors:

1. **Fixed rate, no surprises.** At 5.17% fixed, debt service is locked in for the entire 7-year hold. Interest rates can double — our debt service doesn't move. This eliminates a major source of risk that has crushed floating-rate and bridge loan deals since 2022.

2. **No refinancing event during hold.** Bridge loans and construction loans require refinancing — typically at loan maturity. That refinancing risk has been the primary cause of distress in multifamily over the past two years. Our Fannie Mae loan matures at the projected exit, not in the middle of the hold.

3. **48-month interest-only period.** During the first four years of the hold, we pay interest only — no principal amortization. This maximizes cash flow during the critical period when we're capturing loss-to-lease, burning off concessions, and optimizing other income. IO debt is the reason Year 1–4 cash-on-cash returns are strong even as we execute the business plan.

4. **70% LTV.** A 30% equity cushion between our loan and property value. Property values would have to decline 30% before the loan exceeds asset value — a scenario that would require a 2008-style collapse in a submarket with structural supply constraints and $70B+ in employer investment commitments.

## Rate Buydown — $875,000 (2.0%)

Gray Capital paid $875,000 at closing to buy down the interest rate to 5.17%. This is included in the total uses of funds ($65,832,000).

**Why buy it down?**
- Lower rate increases Year 1 cash flow and early-year distributions
- Improves DSCR headroom during the critical lease rollover period (46% of leases expire by August 2026)
- Every basis point of rate reduction flows directly to distributable cash flow across the IO period

**Net effect:** The buydown cost is recovered quickly through higher early-year distributions, and the fixed rate provides certainty for the full hold period.

## DSCR Analysis

**Going-in DSCR of 1.03x** reflects Year 1 metrics at current occupancy and rents — before concession burn-off, loss-to-lease capture, or income optimization.

| Year | NOI (with Abatement) | Annual Debt Service | DSCR |
|------|---------------------|---------------------|------|
| Y1 | $3.86M | $2.26M | ~1.71x |
| Y2 | $4.07M | $2.26M | ~1.80x |
| Y3 | $4.25M | $2.26M | ~1.88x |
| Y4 | $4.43M | $2.26M | ~1.96x |
| Y5* | $4.61M | ~$2.65M | ~1.74x |
| Y6* | $3.78M | ~$2.65M | ~1.43x |
| Y7* | $3.95M | ~$2.65M | ~1.49x |

*Years 5–7 reflect transition to amortizing debt service after 48-month IO period ends.*

**Note:** The "going-in 1.03x DSCR" cited in offering materials reflects the **pre-abatement NOI** divided by debt service — the conservative metric used by Fannie Mae underwriting standards. Including the abatement, actual Year 1 NOI coverage is ~1.71x. The abatement is not counted in the lender's DSCR calculation, making our actual cash flow cushion substantially wider than the stated 1.03x implies.

## Debt Assumptions at Exit

- **Loan maturity:** Aligned with 7-year hold period
- **Prepayment:** Yield maintenance or step-down prepayment penalties may apply at exit — factored into underwriting
- **Exit scenario:** Property sold; buyer arranges new financing (at-exit cap rate / buyer assumptions)
- **No refinancing risk:** Fixed-rate Fannie Mae structure means no maturity mismatch during hold
