## 2. Residential Condo Plat — Additional 37% Tax Savings

### What It Is
Fairmont has an existing **residential condominium plat** structure, which means the property is legally structured as individual residential condominium units rather than a commercial apartment property. This classification results in the property being assessed at **residential millage rates** rather than commercial millage rates.

### What It's Worth
- **37.17% additional real estate tax savings** vs. commercial millage rates
- This savings compounds on top of the 100% abatement
- Combined, the two structures produce significantly more tax efficiency than either alone
- The condo plat is already implemented — no additional action required

### How It Works
Ohio counties assess real estate taxes using different millage rates for residential vs. commercial property. Residential rates are substantially lower. By platting Fairmont as a condominium community (individual unit ownership structures), the property qualifies for residential millage — a structural benefit that is locked in for the life of the investment.

## 3. Cost Segregation (Investor-Level Tax Benefits)

### What It Is
Cost segregation is an IRS-approved engineering study that accelerates depreciation by reclassifying components of a real estate investment from the standard 27.5-year residential depreciation schedule into shorter-lived categories (5, 7, or 15 years). Combined with **bonus depreciation**, this can generate significant passive losses in Year 1 for investors.

### Fairmont's Cost Segregation Profile
- **Asset:** 2025-vintage Class A multifamily — ideal profile for cost segregation
- **Building components eligible for accelerated depreciation:** Personal property (appliances, fixtures, flooring), land improvements (parking lots, landscaping, lighting), building structural components
- **Year 1 depreciation:** Gray Capital will engage a qualified cost segregation firm; specific study results to be provided in investor materials
- **Typical allocation:** 20–30% of asset value may qualify for 5–15 year depreciation schedules

### For Investors
- Cost segregation creates **passive losses** that offset passive income from this and other real estate investments
- Losses may not be deductible against W-2 or ordinary income (depends on your tax status and real estate professional designation)
- **For real estate professionals (IRS definition):** Losses may offset ordinary income
- **Self-directed IRA investors:** Depreciation benefits generally do not apply within tax-deferred retirement accounts

**Gray Capital's approach:** We engage qualified cost segregation engineers on every acquisition. Results and deductibility depend on each investor's individual tax situation.
