# Fairmont Apartments — Deal Overview

## The Opportunity

Gray Capital is acquiring Fairmont Apartments, a 219-unit, 2025-vintage Class A multifamily community in Westerville, Ohio — on the west edge of New Albany, Columbus' most supply-constrained and affluent submarket. This is a brand-new, institutional-quality asset at $285K/door (15–20% below replacement cost), zero capex required, and ~$290K of embedded loss-to-lease poised to capture as leases roll to market.

We are entering as the supply cycle reverses. New deliveries are falling sharply, the submarket's high-income renter base ($102K median HHI within 3 miles) provides durable demand, and major employer catalysts are arriving during our hold period. The asset is protected by fixed-rate agency debt, a premium submarket with structural supply barriers, and the strongest amenity package in the competitive set.

## Why Fairmont

**1. Buy below replacement cost in Columbus' strongest submarket.**
At $285K/door, we're acquiring a 2025-vintage asset at an estimated 15–20% discount to replacement cost ($350K+/door) in a submarket where median household income exceeds $102K, 55% of residents hold bachelor's degrees, and the rent-to-income ratio sits at just 17–18%. No new competitor can undercut our basis, and the spread widens as construction costs inflate. A 15-year property tax abatement provides additional cash flow support through 2041.

**2. Nearly 6% loss-to-lease sitting in the rent roll.**
The developer underpriced leases during the peak supply cycle to fill the building. ~$290K/year of embedded loss-to-lease is captured on natural lease rollover — no renovation required. 46% of leases roll by August 2026. 3BR units are currently 5–8% below submarket; we'll push 5–10% on renewal.

**3. Supply cliff + demand catalyst timing.**
Westerville new deliveries drop sharply in 2026, while Columbus absorption continues to outpace supply. Anduril Arsenal-1 (4,008 jobs, $132K avg salary) is ramping operations near Fairmont. Intel's Ohio campus (~3,000 engineers at $135K+) is planned for 2030–31, though subject to corporate and macroeconomic factors. These are demand catalysts layered on top of our base case — the deal works without them.

**4. Best-in-market amenity package, no CapEx required.**
Two heated pools, TrackMan golf simulator, dual Starbucks coffee bars, private screening room, co-working spaces, 24-hour fitness. This is the asset others will be compared to. Zero capital spend required during the hold period.

**5. Agency debt with no refinancing risk.**
$43.75M Fannie Mae loan via Greystone at 5.17% fixed, 48 months of interest-only, 7-year term aligned with exit. No floating rate exposure, no bridge loan maturity event, no rate cap expiration. In an environment where floating-rate bridge deals have created distress across the industry, Fairmont's debt structure is built for stability. Gray Capital self-manages through Gray Residential, capturing the management margin directly.

## The Exit

Underwritten exit at 5.50% cap rate in June 2033. At exit, ~8 years of abatement remain — representing an estimated $7–8M NPV to the next buyer. That's a structural pricing floor no unabated comparable carries. At 6.00% exit cap (50 bps of widening), the deal still clears 12%+ IRR.

**Exit cap sensitivity:**
- 5.25% (Bull): ~$86M+ sale price (~$393K+/door)
- 5.50% (Base): ~$82.7M (~$378K/door)
- 5.75% (Bear): ~$79.2M (~$362K/door)
