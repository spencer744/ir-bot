# Fairmont Apartments — Sensitivity Analysis & Scenario Context

## Overview

Gray Capital models multiple scenarios for every deal — not to show a range of optimistic outcomes, but to understand the deal's resilience. For Fairmont, the primary variables driving IRR are:
1. **Rent growth rate** (most impactful on NOI growth)
2. **Exit cap rate** (most impactful on sale proceeds)
3. **Occupancy** (drives revenue)
4. **Tax abatement continuity** (most significant binary risk)

## Conservative Scenario

**Assumptions:**
- Rent growth: 3.0% blended annually (below market historical)
- Exit cap: 5.75% (25 bps above base case)
- Occupancy: 92.5% stabilized economic occupancy
- Concession burn-off: partial (some concessions persist through Year 2)
- Loss-to-lease: captured at 80% of projected rate
- Abatement: remains in place throughout hold

**Key takeaway:** The deal still generates 12%+ LP IRR with meaningful margin above the 8% preferred return, and 2.0x equity multiple (doubling LP capital). This scenario assumes Columbus Class A rents stay flat at current depressed levels with no correction — an unlikely but possible outcome.

**What makes it work:** The tax abatement generates ~$895K–$1.1M/year in cash flow regardless of rent growth. At 3% rent growth with the abatement in place, the deal is serviceable with comfortable distributable cash flow throughout the hold. The base case rent growth thesis adds upside on top of a structurally sound baseline.

## Abatement Loss Scenario (Tail Risk)

**This is the deal's primary binary risk. Gray Capital takes it seriously.**

If the 15-year, 100% tax abatement is lost due to CRA non-compliance:
- LP IRR collapses from base case to approximately **~8.0%** (870 bps reduction)
- NPV loss: $12.2M (full abatement NPV gone)
- IRR remains above the 8% preferred return in this scenario, but barely — and accrued pref may exceed available distributions

**What triggers loss:** One year of CRA non-compliance triggers **permanent, irrecoverable rescission** through 2041. The compliance requirement: 66 units (30%) restricted at 80% AMI rents.

**Gray Capital's mitigation:**
- Dedicated CRA compliance tracking from Day 1 — not delegated to PM, handled directly by Gray Capital
- Income certification on all 66 restricted units conducted annually
- Annual reporting filed 90 days before the March 31 deadline (not at deadline)
- Ohio-qualified property management team with abatement compliance experience
- Too LLC Phase 2 final approval confirmed as a hard closing condition — no close without it
- Abatement transferability confirmed with notice to municipality

**Gray Capital's assessment:** This is an administrative risk, not an economic one. The 66 AMI units are already leased at 80% AMI rents — the current structure already satisfies the requirement. The risk is operational discipline, which is squarely within our control.

## Exit Cap Rate Sensitivity

The deal is underwritten at a 5.50% exit cap. Here's how IRR moves with cap rate changes:

| Exit Cap Rate | Sale Price | vs. Base | IRR Impact |
|--------------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| 5.25% | ~$86M+ | +$3.3M | +~1.5–2% IRR |
| 5.50% (Base) | ~$82.7M | — | 14–16% IRR |
| 5.75% | ~$79.2M | ($3.5M) | ~12–13% IRR |
| 6.00% | ~$75.9M | ($6.8M) | ~11–12% IRR |
| 6.25% | ~$73M | ($9.7M) | ~9–10% IRR |

**5.50% is already conservative.** The Meridian (Columbus Class A comp) traded at 4.95% — 55 bps tighter than our exit assumption. Allure at Polaris traded at 5.10% — 40 bps tighter. We exit 7 years later when:
- ~8 years of abatement remain (next buyer inherits $7–8M NPV)
- Intel Ohio One is operational or approaching operation
- Westerville supply cliff is multi-year entrenched

**The deal clears 12%+ LP IRR through a 6.00% exit cap** — 50 bps of widening from base, which would imply higher interest rates AND no abatement premium at exit. Both simultaneously seem unlikely.
