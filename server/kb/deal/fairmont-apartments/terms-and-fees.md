## Distribution Waterfall (Step-by-Step)

The waterfall governs all cash flows: ongoing distributions AND final sale proceeds.

**Step 1 — Return of Capital**
100% of available proceeds to LPs until their full original capital contribution is returned.

**Step 2 — Preferred Return**
LPs receive all accrued and unpaid 8% cumulative preferred return, calculated from each LP's capital contribution date. Any shortfall from prior periods carries forward — the pref is cumulative, not "use it or lose it."

**Step 3 — No GP Catch-Up**
Gray Capital does NOT use a catch-up provision. After the 8% pref, profits move immediately to the 80/20 split — LPs capture their proportional share from dollar one of profit above pref.

**Step 4 — Profit Split (Below 15% IRR)**
Profits split **80% LP / 20% GP** on both cash flow distributions and sale proceeds, up to a 15% LP IRR.

**Step 5 — Profit Split (Above 15% IRR)**
Once LP IRR exceeds 15%, remaining profits split **60% LP / 40% GP**. This performance-based tier aligns GP incentives with outperformance — the GP earns a larger share only after LPs have achieved strong returns.

## Gray Capital Alignment Mechanisms

**Why we're aligned with you:**

1. **GP Co-Investment (~10%):** Gray Capital and principals invest meaningful capital alongside LPs. If the deal underperforms, GP loses money too. This is not just LP risk.

2. **Performance-Based Promote:** The GP earns its 20% promote only AFTER investors receive return of capital AND 8% cumulative pref. If we miss those thresholds, we earn nothing above our co-investment.

3. **No Catch-Up:** We don't take a disproportionate share to "catch up" after the pref. 80/20 split starts immediately after pref is satisfied.

4. **Self-Management:** Gray Capital captures the property management margin (~3% of EGR) — our operational success directly drives investor distributions, not just a management fee.

5. **Acquisition Fee (1.5%):** One-time, disclosed upfront, baked into the sources & uses. This is how deals get sourced, underwritten, and closed.

## Accredited Investor Requirement

All Gray Capital offerings are 506(c) — open only to verified accredited investors. Accreditation requires one of:
- Individual annual income $200K+ (or $300K+ jointly with spouse) in prior two years
- Net worth exceeding $1M excluding primary residence
- Series 7, Series 65, or Series 82 license holder

Gray Capital uses third-party accreditation verification. Investors through LLCs, trusts, self-directed IRAs, and Solo 401(k)s are welcome — consult your tax advisor regarding UBTI and retirement account considerations.
