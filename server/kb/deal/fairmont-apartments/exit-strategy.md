# Fairmont Apartments — Exit Strategy

## Primary Exit: Year 7 Sale (June 2033)

Gray Capital's primary exit strategy is a **fee-simple sale in Year 7**, approximately June 2033, after the business plan is fully executed and NOI has compounded to peak levels.

**Underwritten exit metrics:**
- Exit cap rate: 5.50%
- Projected sale price: ~$82.7M (~$378K/door)
- Projected LP equity multiple: 2.2x (on base case)
- Target LP IRR: 14–16%

---

## Why Year 7

**The hold period is calibrated to maximize two compounding advantages:**

### 1. Maximum Abatement Benefit During Hold
The 15-year abatement runs through 2041. Closing in June 2026 and holding 7 years means:
- All 7 years of our hold occur within the abatement window
- The abatement generates $895K+ annually and grows ~3% per year
- Total abatement benefit during hold: ~$7.2M+ (cumulative)
- At exit, **~8 years of abatement remain** for the next buyer

### 2. Intel Ohio One Timing
Intel's $28B Ohio One semiconductor campus targets full operation in 2030–31 — arriving during Years 4–5 of our hold. This is not modeled in our base case, but represents a structural demand catalyst that:
- Directly compresses exit cap rates (institutional buyers price growth)
- Adds 3,000+ engineers at $135K+ avg salary within 15 minutes of Fairmont
- Creates 10,000+ secondary jobs in supply chain, construction, and services
- Positions Fairmont at the epicenter of one of the country's largest single employer investments

**We exit approximately 2 years after Intel's arrival** — when demand has absorbed, lease-up is proven, and institutional buyers have full conviction in the submarket's trajectory.

## Alternative Exit Paths

Gray Capital has flexibility — we don't have to sell in Year 7 if conditions aren't optimal:

**Refinance and Hold:**
If cap rates are elevated in 2033 but NOI is growing, Gray Capital can refinance to return investor capital while retaining the asset through more favorable conditions. The abatement runs through 2041 — holding longer increases the benefit, not decreases it.

**Extended Hold:**
If the sale market is temporarily weak, the property continues generating cash flow. Gray Capital will not sell into a down market just to meet a projected timeline. Monthly distributions continue during any hold extension.

**1031 Exchange (for Investors):**
Investors may be able to defer capital gains by rolling their distributable sale proceeds into a qualifying replacement property through a 1031 exchange. Consult your CPA — this is investor-specific.
