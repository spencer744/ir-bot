## Waterfall Structure

The distribution waterfall for Parkview Commons determines the order in which cash flow and sale proceeds are distributed between the Limited Partners (LPs) and the General Partner (GP). The structure is designed to prioritize investor returns before the sponsor participates in profits.

### Tier 1 — Preferred Return (8% Cumulative)

All distributable cash flow is paid to LPs first until they have received an 8.0% annualized cumulative return on their unreturned capital contributions. The preferred return is cumulative, meaning that if distributions in any period fall short of the 8.0% target, the shortfall accrues and must be made up in future periods before the GP receives any promote.

### Tier 2 — No GP Catch-Up

Parkview Commons does not include a GP catch-up provision. This means there is no tier where the GP receives a disproportionate share of distributions to "catch up" to a target split. Once the 8% cumulative preferred return is satisfied, remaining cash flow moves directly to the 70/30 split.

### Tier 3 — 70/30 LP/GP Profit Split

After the preferred return is fully satisfied, all remaining cash flow (both during operations and at sale) is split 70% to LPs and 30% to the GP. The GP's 30% share of profits above the preferred return is commonly referred to as the "promote" and represents the primary performance-based compensation for the sponsor.

### At Disposition (Sale)

Upon sale of the property, proceeds are distributed in the following order:

1. **Return of Capital:** LPs receive 100% of distributions until all capital contributions have been returned.
2. **Accrued Preferred Return:** LPs receive any accrued and unpaid preferred return (8.0% cumulative).
3. **Profit Split:** Remaining proceeds are split 70% to LPs and 30% to the GP.

### Ongoing Fees (Charged Annually)

| Fee | Rate | Basis | Description |
|-----|------|-------|-------------|
| Asset Management Fee | 0.50% | Effective Gross Income | Covers sponsor oversight, reporting, investor relations, and strategic asset management |
| Property Management Fee | 3.00% | Effective Gross Income | Covers on-site staff, leasing, maintenance coordination, and day-to-day operations |

### Fee Descriptions

- **Acquisition Fee (1.75%):** A one-time fee paid to the GP for sourcing, evaluating, negotiating, and closing the acquisition. Covers the costs of deal origination, due diligence coordination, legal oversight, and closing.

- **Loan Guarantee Fee (0.75%):** Compensates the GP principals for personally guaranteeing the $29.4 million senior loan. The GP provides a completion guarantee and a "bad boy" carve-out guarantee, putting personal assets at risk.

- **Asset Management Fee (0.50%):** An ongoing annual fee for the GP's oversight of the property, including strategic decisions, financial reporting, investor communications, capital project oversight, and disposition planning.

- **Property Management Fee (3.00%):** Paid to Gray Residential for on-site property management including leasing, resident services, maintenance, accounting, and compliance. The 3.0% rate is below the typical market range of 4-8% for comparable properties.

- **Construction Management Fee (5.00%):** Covers the GP's oversight of the $5.46 million renovation program, including contractor selection, scope management, quality control, draw processing, and timeline management.

## GP Co-Investment and Alignment

Gray Capital and its principals are investing approximately **$2,100,000** (14% of total equity) alongside LP investors. This co-investment is made on the same terms and subject to the same waterfall as LP capital. The GP's capital is at risk alongside investor capital and receives no priority over LP distributions.

The 14% GP co-investment is meaningful and demonstrates the sponsor's confidence in the business plan. Combined with the no-catch-up waterfall structure and the fact that the majority of GP compensation comes from the 30% promote (which is only earned after LPs receive their 8% preferred return), the alignment between sponsor and investor interests is strong.
