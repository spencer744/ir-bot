# Parkview Commons — Value-Add Business Plan

## Executive Summary

Parkview Commons presents a compelling value-add opportunity to acquire a well-located, 312-unit Class B multifamily community in Indianapolis at a significant discount to replacement cost. The property was built in 2001 and is in good structural condition but features dated unit interiors and underperforming common areas. Gray Capital's business plan targets an average rent increase from $1,138 to $1,400 per month through a combination of interior renovations, exterior improvements, operational optimization, and professional revenue management over a 24-month execution period.

The total renovation budget is $5,460,000, comprising $4,680,000 for interior unit upgrades ($15,000 per unit) and $780,000 for exterior and common area improvements. Upon stabilization, the business plan is projected to increase NOI from $2.65 million at acquisition to $3.91 million by Year 5, a 47% increase.

## Value-Add Thesis

The core value-add thesis rests on three pillars:

1. **Renovation-Driven Rent Growth:** Interior renovations generate an estimated $172 per month average rent premium. Combined with organic market rent growth ($68) and revenue management optimization ($22), the total rent bridge from $1,138 to $1,400 represents a $262 per month increase per unit.

2. **Operational Efficiency:** Transition to Gray Residential property management brings professional leasing, preventive maintenance programs, revenue management software, and resident retention strategies that reduce turnover, lower operating costs, and optimize pricing.

3. **Exterior and Amenity Upgrades:** The $780,000 exterior improvement budget enhances curb appeal, modernizes common areas, and adds amenities that support premium positioning and higher occupancy.

## Rent Bridge Analysis

The rent bridge illustrates the path from current in-place rents to the projected stabilized rent:

| Component | Monthly Amount | Cumulative |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| Current Average Rent | $1,138 | $1,138 |
| Renovation Premium | +$172 | $1,310 |
| Market Rent Growth (over renovation period) | +$68 | $1,378 |
| Revenue Management Optimization | +$22 | $1,400 |
| **Stabilized Average Rent** | | **$1,400** |

The $172 renovation premium is supported by comparable property data showing renovated units in the submarket achieving rents of $1,385 to $1,420. The $68 market growth component assumes 3.5% annual rent growth over the two-year renovation period. The $22 revenue management component reflects income optimization from professional pricing software, lease-term management, and ancillary income programs.

## Interior Renovation Program

### Scope ($15,000 per unit | $4,680,000 total)

Each renovated unit receives a comprehensive interior upgrade designed to bring finishes in line with Class B+ renovated comparables:

- **Quartz countertops** replacing dated laminate surfaces
- **Stainless steel appliance package** including refrigerator, range, dishwasher, and microwave
- **Luxury vinyl plank (LVP) flooring** throughout all living areas, replacing carpet and dated vinyl
- **Updated lighting package** with modern fixtures and under-cabinet lighting
- **New cabinet hardware** with contemporary pulls and soft-close hinges
- **New bathroom vanities** with updated mirrors, faucets, and fixtures
- **Fresh paint** in a modern neutral color palette
- **New baseboards and trim**

### Timeline and Pace

- **Duration:** 24 months (Months 1 through 24)
- **Pace:** Approximately 13 units per month
- **Approach:** Renovations performed on natural turnover to minimize vacancy loss and avoid resident relocation costs

The 13-unit monthly pace is achievable given the property's historical annual turnover rate and the ability to incentivize lease non-renewals for strategically selected units. Gray Capital's renovation team has executed similar programs across its existing portfolio at comparable or faster pace.

## Gray Residential Operations

Upon acquisition, property management transitions to Gray Residential, providing:

- **Dedicated on-site management team** including property manager, assistant manager, leasing consultants, and maintenance technicians
- **Revenue management software** to optimize pricing across all unit types, floor plans, and lease terms
- **Preventive maintenance program** to reduce emergency repair costs and improve resident satisfaction
- **Resident retention programs** including renewal incentives, community events, and responsive maintenance
- **Online resident portal** for rent payments, maintenance requests, and community communications
- **Ancillary income programs** including pet rent, covered parking premiums, storage rentals, and utility billing (RUBS)

## NOI Projections (Base Case)

| Year | Projected NOI | Annual Change |
|------|--------------|---------------|
| Year 0 (Acquisition) | $2,650,000 | — |
| Year 1 | $2,450,000 | -7.5% (renovation disruption) |
| Year 2 | $2,680,000 | +9.4% |
| Year 3 | $2,920,000 | +9.0% |
| Year 4 | $3,050,000 | +4.5% |
| Year 5 | $3,180,000 | +4.3% |

Note: Year 1 NOI dips below acquisition-year NOI due to renovation-related vacancy loss, concessions during the transition period, and initial capital expenditure timing. NOI recovers in Year 2 as renovated units begin leasing at premium rents and stabilizes by Year 3. By Year 5, NOI reaches $3.18 million under the base case, with the property's full stabilized potential of $3.91 million reflecting continued rent growth and full renovation completion.

Annual debt service is $2.02 million per year on the $29.4 million fixed-rate loan at 5.5%, providing positive cash flow in every year of the hold period. The gap between NOI and debt service widens each year as the renovation program drives income growth while debt service remains fixed.

## Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Renovation cost overruns | Contingency built into renovation budget; fixed-price contractor agreements |
| Slower lease-up | Base case assumes 94% occupancy, below renovated comps at 95-96%; conservative cushion |
| Rent premiums below target | Comp analysis supports $1,385-$1,420 renovated rents; our $1,400 target is mid-range |
| Construction delays | Experienced renovation team; phased approach allows flexibility |
| Interest rate exposure | Fixed-rate financing at 5.5% eliminates rate risk during hold |
| Economic downturn | Workforce housing in affordable market; breakeven occupancy at ~82% |
