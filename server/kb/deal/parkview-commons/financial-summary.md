## Four-Scenario Return Analysis

Gray Capital underwrites four scenarios to illustrate the range of potential outcomes. Each scenario varies the key assumptions of annual rent growth, exit capitalization rate, and stabilized occupancy.

| Scenario | Rent Growth | Exit Cap | Occupancy | Target IRR | Equity Multiple | Avg CoC |
|----------|-------------|----------|-----------|------------|-----------------|---------|
| **Conservative** | 2.0% | 5.75% | 91% | ~11-12% | ~1.60-1.70x | ~5.5-6.0% |
| **Base** | 3.5% | 5.25% | 94% | 15.8% | 1.95x | 7.2% |
| **Upside** | 4.5% | 4.80% | 96% | ~19-21% | ~2.25-2.40x | ~8.5-9.5% |
| **Strategic** | 5.5% | 4.50% | 97% | ~22-25% | ~2.55-2.75x | ~10-11% |

### Scenario Descriptions

- **Conservative:** Reflects a soft economic environment with below-trend rent growth (2.0%), elevated vacancy (91% occupancy), and cap rate expansion to 5.75% at exit. Even in this scenario, investors are projected to receive a return of capital plus a meaningful return.

- **Base Case:** Represents Gray Capital's underwritten expectations based on current market data, the renovation business plan, and historical submarket performance. Assumes 3.5% annual rent growth (in line with current Indianapolis trends), 94% stabilized occupancy (below renovated comp average of 95-96%), and a 5.25% exit cap rate.

- **Upside:** Assumes the renovation program outperforms, market conditions remain strong, and the property achieves near-top-of-market occupancy at 96%. Exit cap rate of 4.80% reflects continued cap rate compression or a favorable sale environment.

- **Strategic:** Represents the high end of reasonable outcomes with 5.5% annual rent growth, 97% occupancy, and a 4.50% exit cap rate. This scenario would require exceptionally strong market tailwinds and business plan outperformance.

## Sensitivity Highlights

- **Exit cap rate** is the single largest driver of total returns. A 25 basis point increase in exit cap rate reduces the IRR by approximately 1.5-2.0 percentage points. A 25 basis point decrease adds a similar amount.
- **Rent growth** compounds over the 5-year hold. Each 1% change in annual rent growth impacts the IRR by approximately 2-3 percentage points.
- **Occupancy** directly affects both cash flow and exit value. Each 1% change in stabilized occupancy impacts annual NOI by approximately $50,000-$60,000.

## Breakeven Analysis

| Metric | Breakeven Level | Base Case | Cushion |
|--------|----------------|-----------|---------|
| **Breakeven Occupancy** | ~82% | 94% | 12 points |
| **Breakeven Rent Growth** | ~0.5% (for IRR below pref) | 3.5% | 3.0 points |

The breakeven occupancy of approximately 82% represents the level at which rental income covers operating expenses and debt service with no cash flow remaining for distributions. At the base case 94% occupancy, there is a 12-percentage-point cushion above breakeven, providing meaningful downside protection. The property can sustain a significant decline in occupancy before investors' preferred return is at risk.
