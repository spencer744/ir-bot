## Investment Thesis

Parkview Commons is a 312-unit Class B garden-style multifamily community situated on 22 acres in Indianapolis' desirable northeast corridor. Built in 2001, the property features 2- and 3-story buildings with an average unit size of 956 square feet and a current occupancy of 94%. Despite its strong location and solid construction, the property has received minimal capital investment over the past decade, resulting in dated unit interiors and common areas. Current in-place rents average approximately $1,138 per month, which is 12-15% below renovated comparable properties in the immediate submarket.

The investment thesis is straightforward and repeatable: acquire a well-located, institutional-quality asset below replacement cost, execute a proven interior and exterior renovation program, implement professional revenue management through Gray Residential, and drive NOI growth through meaningful rent increases and operational efficiency. The $42 million purchase price equates to just $134,615 per unit, well below the estimated replacement cost for comparable new construction in the Indianapolis MSA. The property's strong bones, favorable unit mix across four floor plans, and extensive amenity base (including pool, fitness center, clubhouse, dog park, playground, walking trails, and business center) provide an excellent foundation for the value-add program.

Indianapolis offers a compelling macro backdrop for multifamily investment. The metro area has a population of approximately 2.15 million, a cost of living index of 92 (8% below the national average), median household income of $62,400, and unemployment at just 3.4%. The economy is well diversified across healthcare, logistics, government and education, manufacturing, and technology. Importantly, the supply-demand balance remains favorable: annual deliveries of approximately 3,500 to 4,800 units are being met by absorption of 3,800 to 5,100 units, and new supply is overwhelmingly Class A luxury product at substantially higher rent points, limiting direct competitive pressure on the value-add segment.

## Why Parkview Commons

Parkview Commons combines several attributes that make it an attractive risk-adjusted investment. The property's 2001 vintage means it has modern infrastructure (individual HVAC, copper plumbing, adequate electrical) that reduces capital expenditure risk compared to older Class C assets. Its 22-acre campus with extensive amenities provides a resident experience that is difficult for new supply to replicate at comparable rent points. The four-floor-plan unit mix (one-, two-, and three-bedrooms) appeals to a broad renter demographic, from young professionals to families. And the Indianapolis market's affordability, job growth, and landlord-friendly regulatory environment provide a stable foundation for the five-year business plan.

The breakeven occupancy for debt service and operating expenses is approximately 82%, providing a meaningful cushion below the 94% base case stabilized occupancy assumption. This margin of safety, combined with fixed-rate financing and a phased renovation approach, is designed to protect investor capital across a range of market conditions.
