# Gray Residential -- Operational Playbook

## Overview

Gray Residential is Gray Capital's in-house property management subsidiary, managing 7,500+ units across 17 active projects. This playbook covers operational systems for maximizing property performance across Indiana, Ohio, Illinois, Iowa, and Michigan.

---

## Leasing and Revenue Management

### Dynamic Pricing
- **Market Surveys:** Regular competitive surveys of asking rents, concessions, occupancy, and amenities at competing communities
- **Demand-Based Adjustments:** Real-time pricing based on supply/demand signals -- increase during strong demand, adjust to maintain occupancy during soft periods
- **Lease Expiration Management:** Spread expirations across the year, concentrating in peak months (May-September) to avoid off-peak turnover (November-February)
- **Renewal Pricing:** Balances revenue growth with retention by analyzing turnover cost vs. potential rent increase per unit

### Leasing Operations
- Optimized listings on Apartments.com, Zillow, etc., plus property-specific websites
- Same-day response target for inquiries; tour within 48 hours
- Consultative, non-pressured leasing approach with model/show-ready units
- Application approval within 24-48 hours (credit, income, rental history, background checks)

### Ancillary Revenue
- Pet rent/fees, reserved/covered parking, storage rental
- RUBS (ratio utility billing for water, sewer, trash)
- Package lockers, late fees, admin fees

## Maintenance and Capital Expenditure

### Preventive Maintenance (led by Joe Powell, Director of Facilities)
- **HVAC:** Quarterly filter changes, biannual inspections, refrigerant/coil/thermostat checks
- **Plumbing:** Annual water heater inspection, supply line checks during turns, leak monitoring
- **Roofing:** Biannual inspections (spring/fall) for shingles, flashing, drainage
- **Exterior/Common Areas:** Monthly inspections of parking, sidewalks, lighting, signage, landscaping
- **Unit Turns:** Comprehensive inspection checklist covering all systems and finishes

### Work Order Management
1. **Submission:** Online portal, phone, or in-person; emergencies escalated immediately
2. **Triage:** Supervisor assigns by priority and technician skill
3. **Completion:** Technician documents and closes in system
4. **Follow-Up:** High-priority orders get resident satisfaction check
5. **Reporting:** Volume, response times, completion rates tracked portfolio-wide

**Response Targets:** Emergency: same-day (4 hrs) | Urgent (HVAC, plumbing, appliances): 24 hrs | Routine: 48-72 hrs

### Capital Expenditure Planning
Informed by acquisition condition assessments, ongoing preventive inspections, budget forecasts for major systems (roofing, HVAC, parking, siding, windows), and market positioning objectives. CapEx evaluated on NOI impact, resident satisfaction, and long-term asset value.

## Resident Experience

Resident satisfaction drives financial performance: higher renewals reduce turnover costs, referrals reduce marketing costs, and positive reviews attract qualified prospects.

- **Move-In:** Welcome communication with community info, maintenance instructions, key contacts; units inspected to high standard
- **Community Events:** Seasonal gatherings, food trucks, holiday celebrations, resident appreciation
- **Communication:** Email updates, newsletters, social media; advance notice of maintenance and improvements
- **Online Reputation:** Active monitoring and response on Google, Apartments.com, social media

### Lease Renewal Process (begins 90-120 days before expiration)
1. Renewal offer with proposed rent based on market conditions and retention analysis
2. Personal outreach from community manager
3. Negotiation within defined parameters (longer term or modest concession)
4. Notice period for non-renewals to begin pre-marketing

## Renovation Coordination

Gray Residential works with Gray Construction & Design for value-add renovations:

1. **Unit Selection:** Based on lease expiration timing, condition, market positioning; renovated during natural turnover
2. **Scope:** Kitchen upgrades, bathroom updates, LVP flooring, lighting/hardware, paint
3. **Execution:** 2-4 weeks per unit; maintenance team handles make-ready, construction team handles renovation
4. **Quality Inspection** before release to leasing
5. **Lease-Up** at target premium rent
6. **Tracking:** Costs, timelines, and achieved premiums tracked at unit level

**Minimizing Disruption:** Designated construction hours, accessible common areas, advance resident notification, maintained maintenance service levels.
