# Gray Capital -- Investment Case Studies

## Case Study 1: Villas on Fir -- Core-Plus Acquisition in Granger, Indiana

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Property | Villas on Fir |
| Location | Granger, Indiana |
| Units | 290 |
| Acquisition Price | $61.25 million |
| Strategy | Core-Plus |
| Target Cash-on-Cash | 15% |
| Projected IRR | 30%+ |
| Status | Active |

### Market Context
Granger is an affluent community in St. Joseph County, northern Indiana. Economy anchored by healthcare (Beacon Health, Memorial Hospital), education (Notre Dame, IU South Bend), and manufacturing. High household incomes, strong schools, and limited multifamily supply pipeline due to land/zoning constraints -- supporting rent growth for existing communities.

### Investment Thesis
Unlike typical value-add deals, Villas on Fir was already well-maintained and near stabilization. Core-Plus strategy focuses on operational efficiency, revenue management, and targeted common area/amenity improvements rather than wholesale unit renovations.

**Key Value Drivers:**
1. **Immediate Cash Flow:** 15% target CoC from day one (vs. typical 8% for value-add), reflecting strong in-place income and conservative financing
2. **Revenue Optimization:** Dynamic pricing, lease expiration management, ancillary income (pet fees, parking, storage), RUBS utility billing -- no significant CapEx required
3. **Selective Improvements:** Fitness center upgrades, outdoor gathering spaces, landscaping, signage/branding -- improve positioning without unit renovation cost/disruption
4. **Operational Efficiency:** Gray Residential assumed management, implementing centralized revenue management, preventive maintenance, and retention programs

### Current Status
Post-acquisition: management transitioned, dynamic pricing implemented, amenity improvements underway. Occupancy strong, revenue per unit trending above underwriting. Conservative scenario still projects returns above minimum thresholds.

## Case Study 2: Club Meridian -- Value-Add Acquisition in Okemos, Michigan

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Property | Club Meridian |
| Location | Okemos, Michigan |
| Units | 406 |
| Acquisition Price | $70.5 million |
| Strategy | Value-Add (Fund) |
| Projected NOI Increase | 27.5% |
| Status | Active |

### Market Context
Okemos is a Lansing suburb. Lansing-East Lansing metro (~480,000 pop.) driven by state government, Michigan State University, healthcare (Sparrow Health, McLaren Greater Lansing), and a growing tech sector. Dual government/university anchors provide recession-resistant employment. Okemos has top-ranked schools (Okemos Community Schools) and limited development land, creating persistent premium rental demand.

### Investment Thesis
Acquired through Gray Capital's fund vehicle from an operator who had deferred capital improvements and operational optimization. Business plan targets 27.5% NOI increase through:

1. **Interior Renovations:** Kitchen upgrades (countertops, cabinetry, appliances, fixtures), bathroom modernization, new flooring, lighting, W/D connections. Projected $150-$250/month premiums per renovated unit
2. **Exterior/Common Improvements:** New signage/branding, parking resurfacing, landscaping, renovated clubhouse/leasing center, upgraded fitness center, outdoor amenities, improved lighting/security
3. **Operational Turnaround:** Below-market occupancy and rents, above-benchmark expenses under prior management. New on-site team, dynamic pricing, vendor renegotiation, preventive maintenance
4. **Ancillary Revenue:** Pet rent/fees, parking premiums, storage, RUBS, package management

### Current Status
Gray Residential management installed, operational changes underway. Interior renovation program commenced -- initial units leased at or above projected premiums, validating the scope. Held within Gray Capital's fund alongside Crosswinds (Indianapolis), Sycamore Terrace (Terre Haute), and Echo Park (Bloomington).

### Key Takeaways
- **Scale:** 406 units/$70.5M demonstrates institutional-scale execution capability
- **Geographic Expansion:** Continued push into adjacent Midwest markets beyond Indiana
- **Fund Strategy:** Diversified multi-property exposure through single investment vehicle
- **Vertical Integration:** Coordinating 406-unit renovation alongside operational turnaround showcases integrated platform
