# Gray Residential -- Property Management Platform

## Overview

Gray Residential is Gray Capital's in-house property management subsidiary, managing 7,500+ units across 17 active projects. Launched in 2018 after Spencer Gray experienced misaligned incentives with third-party managers (prioritizing fees over NOI, deferring capital conversations, lacking urgency on business plan execution). By building Gray Residential, every property is managed by a team solely focused on executing Gray Capital's business plans for investors.

Led by Katrina Greene (Director of Property Management) and Joe Powell (Director of Facilities), supported by regional managers, community managers, leasing consultants, maintenance technicians, and admin staff.

---

## Leadership

### Katrina Greene -- Director of Property Management
Oversees leasing, revenue management, resident relations, and site-level management across the portfolio. Sets/achieves occupancy and revenue targets, manages renewals, and maintains standards for professionalism and resident experience.

Philosophy: best-performing communities are where residents feel valued -- satisfied residents renew more, refer friends, take better care of homes. This translates to lower turnover/make-ready costs, higher occupancy, and more consistent revenue.

**Standardized Operating Model:**
- Centralized revenue management informed by market data and portfolio-wide demand trends
- Standardized leasing processes with prompt follow-up and thorough qualification
- Resident retention programs (proactive outreach, events, responsive maintenance)
- Performance accountability via KPIs: occupancy, renewal rates, revenue/unit, expenses, work order times, satisfaction scores

Works with investment team during underwriting to validate leasing assumptions, staffing plans, and operational risks. Leads management transitions on new acquisitions.

### Joe Powell -- Director of Facilities
Oversees physical condition, preventive maintenance, and capital planning for every property. Manages maintenance teams, vendor relationships, and quality/safety standards.

**Facilities Program:**
- **Preventive Maintenance:** HVAC filters quarterly, roofs biannually, plumbing checked during turns, common areas monthly
- **Work Order Management:** Technology-tracked from submission through completion. Monitors volume, response times, completion rates portfolio-wide
- **Vendor Management:** In-house technicians for routine work; vetted vendors for specialized services (elevators, fire safety, pest control, landscaping)
- **Capital Planning:** Tracks condition/useful life of major systems to anticipate future needs and budget accordingly
- **Construction Coordination:** During value-add renovations, coordinates with Alex Gray's team to maintain resident service quality

Also conducts physical inspections of prospective acquisitions, providing detailed deferred maintenance assessments, capital needs, and staffing requirements for underwriting.

## Operating Model

### 1. Centralized Oversight with Local Execution
Strategic decisions (pricing, marketing, capital allocation, vendors) made centrally. Tactical execution (tours, maintenance, resident interactions) handled by experienced site-level teams.

### 2. Technology-Enabled Operations
Industry-leading property management software for accounting, leasing, maintenance, and communications. Online resident portals for rent payment and maintenance requests. Real-time portfolio performance data and reporting.

### 3. Standardized Processes with Continuous Improvement
Documented SOPs for leasing, move-in, maintenance, renewal, move-out, make-ready, and eviction -- refined from years of experience across thousands of units.

### 4. Alignment with Investment Objectives
Unlike third-party managers who manage for fees, Gray Residential manages for investment returns. Every operational decision considers the investment thesis: What maximizes long-term NOI? What renovation scope commands the best premium-to-cost ratio? This is the fundamental advantage of vertical integration.
