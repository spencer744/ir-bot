# Spencer Gray -- Market Views & Outlook

*Reflects Spencer Gray's market analysis from podcast appearances, The Gray Report, and investor communications.*

---

## The 2026 Multifamily Landscape

The market is finding new equilibrium after significant 2020-2025 volatility (pandemic uncertainty, explosive rent growth, aggressive rate hikes, new supply wave, transaction dislocation).

**Transaction Volume Recovery:** Bid-ask spread that paralyzed the 2023-2024 market has narrowed. Sellers accept adjusted cap rates; buyers comfortable with current rate environment. Gray Capital sees opportunity to deploy capital into previously unavailable deals.

**Rent Growth Normalization:** After double-digit growth in 2021-2022, growth has moderated. Highly market-dependent: Sun Belt metros with aggressive new supply see flat/negative growth. Midwest markets with constrained supply maintain positive growth and stable occupancy.

**Supply Cycle Peaking:** New deliveries reaching peak in many markets. Construction starts dropped when rates rose, shrinking the future pipeline. Positive for existing owners: competitive pressure on rents/occupancy will ease over 12-24 months.

---

## Interest Rates and Capital Markets

Rates moderated from 2023-2024 peaks but remain well above 2020-2021 near-zero levels. The era of ultra-cheap debt is over; deals must work with current financing costs.

**Key Impacts:**
1. **Higher Debt Costs:** Properties acquired in 2020-2022 with short-term floating-rate debt face significant refinancing stress
2. **Cap Rate Adjustment:** Higher rates pushed cap rates up / values down from 2022 peaks -- opportunity for new buyers, reality check for existing owners
3. **Leverage Discipline:** Sponsors with 80%+ LTV floating-rate bridge loans facing distress; those with 65-75% LTV fixed-rate/rate-capped debt are well-positioned

**Gray Capital's Positioning:** Prefers fixed-rate agency financing or floating-rate with rate caps. Conservative leverage. Every deal stress-tested for higher-than-current rates under Conservative scenario.

## Midwest vs. Sun Belt

Data from 2023-2025 strongly supports the Midwest thesis.

**Sun Belt Challenges:**
- **Oversupply:** Easy permitting and available land drove construction surges in Austin, Phoenix, Dallas, Atlanta, Charlotte, Nashville, Jacksonville
- **Rent Compression:** Rents flattened or declined as new supply competes for tenants
- **Insurance Costs:** Climate-related events drove dramatic property insurance increases, eroding NOI

**Midwest Resilience:**
- **Constrained Supply:** Higher construction costs relative to rents, less available land, more conservative lending limits new development
- **Stable Occupancy:** Consistently high levels from growing, diversifying employment base
- **Positive Rent Growth:** Maintained through the cycle via favorable supply-demand dynamics
- **Lower Insurance Costs:** Structural operating expense advantage vs. coastal/Sun Belt
- **Favorable Basis:** Lower per-unit acquisition costs provide larger margin of safety and higher day-one cash yields

## 2026 Watch List

1. **Supply Pipeline:** Declining construction starts translating to fewer future deliveries -- bullish for existing owners
2. **Employment/Wage Growth:** Midwest diversification into healthcare, tech, logistics, advanced manufacturing supports rent growth
3. **Interest Rate Direction:** Monitoring Fed policy for refinancing opportunities; protected against stability/increases via conservative leverage
4. **Distressed Opportunities:** Sponsors with aggressive leverage/short-term debt from 2020-2022 may need to sell at discounts as loans mature
5. **Insurance/Operating Costs:** Trending upward nationally; firm's 7,500+ unit scale provides procurement advantages
6. **Migration/Demographics:** Domestic migration favors affordable, high-quality-of-life metros; remote work broadens potential resident pool for Midwest cities

## 2026 Investment Posture

- **Selective Acquisitions:** Active but maintaining strict pricing/underwriting discipline
- **Value-Add Focus:** Targeting 15%+ IRR and 8%+ CoC through renovations and operational improvements
- **Geographic Expansion:** Selectively into adjacent Midwest markets (Dayton, Champaign, Des Moines, Okemos)
- **Distressed Opportunities:** Monitoring for quality assets at discounted pricing
- **Debt Strategy:** Locking fixed-rate financing, conservative leverage, pursuing refinancing where favorable
