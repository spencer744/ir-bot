# Spencer Gray -- CEO & Founder, Gray Capital

## Background and Career Path

Spencer Gray is the founder and Chief Executive Officer of Gray Capital, a vertically integrated multifamily investment firm headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana. Under Spencer's leadership, Gray Capital has grown from a single acquisition into a platform that has transacted over $2 billion in multifamily real estate, acquired more than 7,500 apartment units, and delivered a realized track record that includes a 29.3% average IRR across 10 full-cycle deals with zero capital losses.

Spencer's path to multifamily investing was driven by a deep study of real estate economics and a conviction that apartment investing represents the optimal asset class for building long-term wealth. He recognized early in his career that multifamily real estate offers a unique combination of attributes unavailable in other asset classes: necessity-based demand (everyone needs housing), inflation protection (monthly lease rollovers allow rents to track inflation), significant tax advantages (depreciation and cost segregation), forced appreciation (the ability to increase property value through operational improvements), and scalable cash flow.

Before launching Gray Capital, Spencer studied the multifamily landscape extensively and concluded that while institutional capital was flowing primarily into coastal and Sun Belt markets, the Midwest offered a fundamentally more attractive risk-return profile. Lower per-unit basis, stronger cash yields, favorable rent-to-income ratios, and less institutional competition meant that a disciplined operator could generate superior risk-adjusted returns. He founded Gray Capital to execute on this thesis.

## Investment Philosophy

Spencer's investment philosophy can be distilled into several core principles that guide every decision at Gray Capital:

**Conservative Underwriting is Non-Negotiable.** Spencer insists on underwriting every deal across four scenarios: Conservative, Base, Aggressive, and Home Run. The Conservative scenario is genuinely conservative -- it assumes below-market rent growth, higher vacancy, increased expenses, and unfavorable exit conditions. A deal must generate acceptable returns at the Conservative level to proceed. This discipline has produced a track record with zero capital losses.

**Vertical Integration Drives Execution.** Spencer built Gray Capital as a vertically integrated platform because he believes that controlling property management, construction, and capital management under one umbrella eliminates the misaligned incentives and execution failures that plague operators who rely on third parties. When the investment team underwrites a renovation budget, Gray Construction & Design executes it. When the asset management team identifies a leasing opportunity, Gray Residential implements it.

**The Midwest is Structurally Advantaged.** Spencer has been a vocal advocate for Midwest multifamily investing at a time when many investors are focused on Sun Belt markets. He argues that lower per-unit basis, stronger cash yields, and less competition create a better risk-return profile -- especially for investors who prioritize capital preservation alongside growth. He frequently discusses this thesis in podcast appearances and through The Gray Report.

**Transparency Builds Trust.** Spencer publishes Gray Capital's complete track record, including every deal and every return metric. He sends quarterly reports with property-level detail, regular video updates, and maintains an open-door policy with investors. He believes that the syndication industry suffers from a lack of transparency and that sponsors who are willing to operate openly will build the strongest long-term investor relationships.

**Alignment of Interest is Essential.** Spencer and the Gray Capital team invest alongside limited partners in every deal, with an average sponsor co-investment of 14% of total equity. Spencer believes that meaningful co-investment is the single most important structural feature of a real estate syndication because it ensures that the sponsor's financial incentives are aligned with those of investors.
