# FAQ: Fees, Promote Structure & Alignment

**Q: What fees does Gray Capital charge on the Parkview Commons deal?**

A: All fees are disclosed in the PPM. The fee schedule for Parkview Commons:

| Fee | Rate | Basis | Timing |
|-----|------|-------|--------|
| Acquisition Fee | 1.75% | Purchase price | One-time at closing |
| Loan Guarantee Fee | 0.75% | Loan amount | One-time at closing |
| Asset Management Fee | 0.50% | Effective gross income | Ongoing, annual |
| Property Management Fee | 3.0% | Effective gross income | Ongoing, annual |
| Construction Management Fee | 5.0% | Renovation budget | During renovation period |

**Q: What is the acquisition fee and why is it charged?**

A: The acquisition fee is 1.75% of the purchase price, charged once at closing. For Parkview Commons ($42M purchase price), this is $735,000. It compensates the sponsor for sourcing, underwriting, and closing the deal -- Gray Capital evaluates hundreds of deals for every one that closes. The fee covers earnest money, due diligence, legal costs, and market research. It is deducted from capital raise proceeds and factored into projected returns.

**Q: What is the loan guarantee fee?**

A: The loan guarantee fee is 0.75% of the loan amount, paid once at closing. It compensates the GP principals for personally guaranteeing the mortgage loan, exposing them to liability in the event of default or bad-boy carve-outs. LPs are not personally liable for the debt -- their risk is limited to invested capital.

**Q: What does the asset management fee cover?**

A: The asset management fee is 0.50% of effective gross income annually, covering the GP's strategic oversight: monitoring financial performance, overseeing the property management team, managing lender relationships, directing capital improvements, producing quarterly reports and investor communications, and evaluating exit timing. At 0.50%, this is well below the industry standard of 1-2%.

**Q: What is the property management fee and who receives it?**

A: The property management fee is 3.0% of effective gross income -- an operating expense paid to the property management team (not a GP fee). It covers leasing, maintenance, tenant relations, rent collection, and vendor management. Gray Capital's vertically integrated platform provides in-house management for direct operational control. The 3.0% rate is competitive with the 3-5% industry standard.

**Q: What is the construction management fee?**

A: 5.0% of the total renovation budget, covering contractor procurement, project scheduling, quality control, budget tracking, and change order management. Gray Capital's in-house construction team executes renovations directly for better cost control. This fee is included in the renovation budget and factored into projected returns.

**Q: How does the promote (carried interest) work?**

A: The promote is the GP's share of profits above the preferred return. After investors receive their 8% cumulative preferred return and full return of capital, remaining profits are split 70/30 (LP/GP). The GP's 30% share is the promote. If the deal fails to meet the 8% preferred return, the GP earns no promote.

**Q: Can you walk through the waterfall step by step?**

A: The Parkview Commons distribution waterfall:

1. **Return of Capital (100% to LPs)** -- LPs receive their entire original capital back first.
2. **Preferred Return (100% to LPs)** -- LPs receive any accrued/unpaid 8% cumulative preferred return, calculated from each LP's contribution date.
3. **No GP Catch-Up** -- No catch-up provision. The GP does not receive a disproportionate allocation before the profit split begins.
4. **Profit Split (70% LP / 30% GP)** -- All remaining profits split 70/30.

This waterfall applies to both monthly distributions and final sale proceeds.

**Q: What does "no GP catch-up" mean and why is it important?**

A: A GP catch-up lets the GP receive 100% of profits in a tranche after the pref is paid, until the GP reaches a target percentage -- reducing what LPs receive. Gray Capital does not use a catch-up. After the 8% pref, profits immediately move to the 70/30 split. This is a more investor-friendly structure.

**Q: How does Gray Capital's co-investment create alignment?**

A: Gray Capital invests an average of 14% of total sponsor co-investment alongside LPs in each deal. The GP team has significant personal capital at risk -- if the deal loses money, the GP loses money. This co-investment, combined with the preferred return and performance-based promote, creates multi-layered alignment.

**Q: Are fees deducted from my investment or from property income?**

A: Acquisition and loan guarantee fees are deducted from capital raise proceeds at closing. Ongoing fees (asset management, property management) come from property operating income. The construction management fee comes from the renovation budget. No fees are billed directly to investors after the initial investment. All fees are reflected in underwritten projections, so target returns (15.8% IRR, 1.95x equity multiple, 7.2% cash-on-cash) are net of all fees.

**Q: Are the projected returns shown before or after fees?**

A: All projected returns -- 15.8% IRR, 1.95x equity multiple, 7.2% cash-on-cash -- are shown net of all fees. Fees are already built into the financial model. The returns represent what LPs are projected to receive after all fees, expenses, and the GP's promote.
