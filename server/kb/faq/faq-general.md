# Frequently Asked Questions -- General

**Q: What is Gray Capital?**

A: Gray Capital is a private equity real estate firm headquartered in Indianapolis, Indiana, specializing in the acquisition and repositioning of multifamily apartment communities across the Midwest. Since inception, Gray Capital has completed over $2 billion in total transaction volume encompassing more than 7,500 apartment units. The firm operates a vertically integrated platform, meaning it handles acquisitions, asset management, property management, and construction management in-house. This structure gives Gray Capital direct control over every phase of the investment lifecycle and allows the team to move quickly when opportunities arise. The firm targets Class A and Class B workforce housing properties in high-growth Midwest submarkets, with a particular focus on the Indianapolis metropolitan area.

**Q: What is Gray Capital's track record?**

A: Gray Capital has realized 10 deals to date, delivering an average internal rate of return (IRR) of 29.3%, an average equity multiple of 2.16x, and zero capital losses across all realized investments. Past performance is not indicative of future results, but this track record reflects the firm's disciplined underwriting approach and hands-on asset management. Additionally, Gray Capital's principals invest an average of 14% of total sponsor co-investment capital alongside limited partners, demonstrating meaningful personal financial alignment with investor outcomes.

**Q: Do I need to be an accredited investor?**

A: Yes. All Gray Capital offerings are structured as 506(c) Regulation D offerings under SEC rules, which require all investors to be verified accredited investors. Generally, this means you must meet one of the following criteria: individual annual income of $200,000 or more (or $300,000 or more jointly with a spouse) in each of the prior two years with a reasonable expectation of the same in the current year, or a net worth exceeding $1 million excluding the value of your primary residence. Certain professional certifications (such as Series 7, Series 65, or Series 82 licenses) also qualify. Under 506(c), Gray Capital is required to take reasonable steps to verify accredited status, which may include reviewing tax returns, bank statements, or obtaining a letter from a CPA, attorney, or registered investment advisor.

**Q: Can I invest through an entity such as an LLC, trust, or IRA?**

A: Yes. Many of our investors invest through LLCs, family trusts, or self-directed IRAs and Solo 401(k) accounts. If you invest through a self-directed retirement account, you will need a custodian that permits alternative investments -- Gray Capital can provide referrals to custodians experienced with real estate syndications. Keep in mind that investing through an IRA or other tax-deferred retirement account changes the tax treatment: depreciation and other pass-through tax benefits typically do not apply within retirement accounts, and UBTI (Unrelated Business Taxable Income) rules may apply if the investment uses leverage. Consult your CPA or tax advisor to understand the implications for your specific situation.
