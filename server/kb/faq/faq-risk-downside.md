# FAQ: Risks, Downside Scenarios & Capital Protection

**Q: What are the primary risks of investing in Fairmont Apartments?**

- **Market Risk:** Economic downturns, job losses, or population shifts in Columbus could reduce rental demand and property values.
- **Execution Risk:** Business plan depends on capturing loss-to-lease, burning off concessions, and implementing RUBS. Lower-than-expected rent growth or higher concessions affect returns.
- **Tax Abatement Risk:** The 15-year abatement is the deal's largest value driver (~$895K/yr). One year of CRA non-compliance triggers permanent rescission through 2041. Gray Capital implements dedicated compliance infrastructure to mitigate this risk.
- **Interest Rate Risk:** Fixed-rate debt at 5.17% protects during hold, but higher cap rates at exit (driven by higher rates) would reduce sale price.
- **Occupancy Risk:** Lower occupancy reduces cash flow. Current 92% occupancy at acquisition; stabilized at 96% peak in first 11 months.
- **Liquidity Risk:** Illiquid private investment; capital committed for projected 7-year hold, possibly longer.
- **Regulatory Risk:** Changes in zoning, codes, rent regulation, or tax policy could impact operations.
- **Force Majeure:** Natural disasters, pandemics, or unforeseen events could disrupt operations.

**Q: Could I lose my entire investment?**

Total loss is unlikely but possible under extreme circumstances (severe depression, catastrophic uninsured damage, sustained high vacancy with inability to service debt, fraud). Debt structured at 65-70% LTV provides 30-35% equity cushion before property is underwater. Review PPM for complete risk factors.

**Q: Has Gray Capital ever lost investor capital?**

No. Across 10 realized deals: 29.3% avg IRR, 2.16x avg equity multiple, zero capital losses. Every realized investment returned full capital plus profits. Past performance does not guarantee future results.

**Q: How does Gray Capital protect against downside risk?**

- **Conservative Underwriting:** Four scenarios (Conservative, Base, Upside, Strategic). Base case represents realistic assumptions, not best case.
- **Fixed-Rate Debt:** 5.17% fixed with 70% LTV eliminates rising interest payment risk during hold.
- **Operating Reserves:** Cover unexpected expenses and short-term cash flow gaps.
- **CapEx Contingency:** Renovation budget includes buffer for cost overruns.
- **Sponsor Co-Investment:** 14% average alongside LPs creates strong alignment.
- **Vertical Integration:** Direct control over acquisitions, asset management, property management, and construction for rapid response to challenges.
- **Insurance:** Comprehensive property, liability, loss-of-rent, and umbrella coverage.

**Q: What happens if occupancy drops significantly?**

Gray Capital underwrites stabilized occupancy conservatively (93.5% economic), providing a cushion. Response: adjust marketing/pricing, evaluate concessions, deploy operating reserves. Fixed-rate debt at 5.17% means no compounding from rising interest payments. Breakeven occupancy is well below underwritten stabilized occupancy. Additionally, the $895K+ annual tax abatement provides a substantial cash flow floor regardless of occupancy fluctuations.

**Q: How does the fixed-rate debt at 5.17% protect investors?**

Three layers: (1) Interest locked for the entire 7-year hold -- debt service stays constant regardless of market rate changes, protecting distributable cash flow. (2) 70% LTV means a 30% equity cushion before the loan exceeds property value. (3) Fannie Mae agency debt eliminates the refinancing risk that has caused widespread distress for floating-rate and bridge loan deals since 2022.

**Q: What happens if there is a recession?**

Multifamily historically shows resilience during downturns -- housing is a necessity, and workforce housing (Class A) can see increased demand as renters trade down. A recession could still impact rent growth, turnover, concessions, or bad debt. Gray Capital stress-tests against recessionary scenarios and sizes reserves accordingly.

**Q: Could Gray Capital issue a capital call?**

No mandatory capital call provision. Maximum exposure is your initial investment. Deals structured with sufficient reserves/contingency. If additional capital is needed: operating cash flow, credit facilities, additional investors, or business plan restructuring -- existing investors not forced to contribute.

**Q: What if the business plan doesn't execute as projected?**

Fairmont requires zero renovation capital. There are no contractors, no renovation timelines, no construction risk. The business plan is: manage renewals at or above market, implement RUBS, optimize parking and pet rent, and maintain compliance on the 66 AMI units. If rent growth underperforms, the 15-year tax abatement (~$895K+/year) provides a cash flow floor that keeps debt service covered and distributions flowing throughout the hold.

**Q: What if Gray Capital cannot sell at projected exit cap rate?**

Gray Capital can extend the hold period for more favorable conditions rather than selling into a down market. Property continues generating cash flow and distributions during extension. Conservative and Base scenarios model different exit cap rate assumptions. No fixed sale date -- flexibility advantage over public markets or closed-end funds.

**Q: What recourse do investors have if the deal underperforms?**

LPs have limited liability -- cannot lose more than initial investment. Operating agreement provides quarterly financials, portal access, and performance transparency. 8% cumulative preferred return accrues before GP earns promote. GP's 14% co-investment means sponsor capital is at risk alongside investors; underperformance reduces/eliminates GP promote.
