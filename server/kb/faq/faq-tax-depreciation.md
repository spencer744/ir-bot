# FAQ: Tax Benefits, Depreciation & K-1s

**Q: What are the tax benefits of investing in Parkview Commons?**

A: As a partnership-structured syndication, tax benefits pass through to investors via Schedule K-1:

- **Accelerated depreciation** via cost segregation study, front-loading deductions into early years
- **Mortgage interest deductions** passed through proportionally
- **Operating expense deductions** for management, insurance, maintenance, etc.
- **Passive loss generation** to offset passive income from other real estate investments
- **Long-term capital gains treatment** on appreciation at sale (held over one year)

Because the LLC is taxed as a partnership, these flow directly to your personal return -- no entity-level taxation.

**Q: What is cost segregation and how does it work?**

A: Cost segregation is an IRS-approved engineering study that reclassifies building components from the standard 27.5-year depreciation schedule into shorter-lived categories of 5, 7, and 15 years (e.g., flooring, cabinetry, appliances, parking lots, landscaping, certain plumbing/electrical). For Parkview Commons, the depreciable basis is approximately $35 million. The study is expected to accelerate about 60% of the depreciable basis into shorter-lived categories, creating substantial paper losses in early years allocated to investors proportionally.

**Q: How much depreciation can I expect in Year 1?**

A: A $100,000 investment is projected to generate approximately $82,000 in paper losses (tax deductions) in Year 1. Your K-1 would show roughly $82,000 in losses against your $100K investment, even though the property generates positive cash flow. This results from accelerated depreciation -- a non-cash deduction. Actual amounts depend on the final cost segregation study, contribution timing, and applicable bonus depreciation rates.

**Q: Can I use these depreciation losses to offset my W-2 income?**

A: It depends. Under IRS Section 469, syndication losses are passive losses. Passive losses can offset passive income (other rentals, syndications, certain businesses where you don't materially participate) but generally cannot offset active income like W-2 wages.

**Exception:** If you or your spouse qualifies as a Real Estate Professional (REP), passive real estate losses may be reclassified as non-passive, potentially offsetting W-2 and other active income. See below for REP details.

If you lack passive income and don't qualify as a REP, unused passive losses carry forward indefinitely and apply against future passive income or are recognized when you dispose of your interest.

**Q: What is Real Estate Professional (REP) status and how does it affect my tax benefits?**

A: A REP meets two IRS requirements: (1) more than 750 hours/year in real estate activities where they materially participate, and (2) more than half of total working hours in real estate. If qualified, Section 469 passive loss limits may not apply, allowing depreciation losses to offset W-2, business, and other active income. This is valuable for high-income investors whose spouses work full-time in real estate. REP status is complex and IRS-scrutinized -- maintain detailed time logs and work with a real estate-specialized CPA. Gray Capital does not determine REP status.

**Q: What is depreciation recapture and how does it affect me at sale?**

A: When the property sells at a gain, previously claimed depreciation is recaptured at 25% (Section 1250) rather than the lower long-term capital gains rate of 15-20%. The remaining gain above original cost basis is taxed at capital gains rates.

Depreciation defers taxes rather than eliminating them, but the deferral is valuable: you receive deductions today at your marginal rate (potentially 32-37%) and pay recapture at 25% years later. Additionally, 1031 exchanges at the entity level can further defer recapture.

**Q: When will I receive my K-1?**

A: Gray Capital targets March 15 delivery for the prior tax year. K-1s are prepared by the partnership's tax accounting firm and delivered electronically via the investor portal. You receive an email notification when available. If you need a tax extension, your CPA can use prior-year estimates as a starting point.

**Q: What information is on the K-1?**

A: Schedule K-1 (Form 1065) reports your share of partnership income, losses, deductions, and credits. Key line items:

- **Box 1:** Ordinary business income/loss
- **Box 2:** Net rental real estate income/loss (where depreciation-driven losses appear)
- **Box 8:** Net short-term capital gain
- **Box 9a:** Net long-term capital gain (at sale)
- **Box 13:** Various deductions including Section 179
- **Box 19:** Distributions received

Your CPA uses the K-1 to complete your Form 1040. Ensure your preparer is experienced with partnership K-1s and passive real estate investments.

**Q: Will this investment affect my state tax filings?**

A: Yes. Since Parkview Commons is in Indiana, you may have an Indiana filing requirement regardless of your home state. Indiana's income tax rate is approximately 3.05%, with some counties imposing additional local taxes. Gray Capital provides state-specific K-1 information. Your CPA can advise on home-state credits for taxes paid to other states.

**Q: Can I invest through a self-directed IRA and still get the tax benefits?**

A: You can invest via a self-directed IRA (Traditional or Roth) or Solo 401(k), but depreciation and passive loss benefits do not apply within tax-deferred/exempt accounts. Additionally, because the partnership uses leverage, a portion of income may be subject to UBTI (Unrelated Business Taxable Income), creating a potential tax liability within the IRA. Consult your CPA and custodian about UBTI before investing through a retirement account.
