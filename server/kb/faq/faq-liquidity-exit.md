# FAQ: Liquidity, Exit Strategy & Hold Period

**Q: How long will my capital be invested?**

A: The projected hold period is five years. Gray Capital targets three to seven years depending on market conditions and business plan execution. The actual hold period may be shorter or longer -- the firm has flexibility to adjust the timeline to maximize investor returns.

**Q: Is this a liquid investment? Can I access my capital before the property is sold?**

A: No. This is an illiquid investment with no public market for LP interests and no on-demand redemptions. Your capital is committed for the hold period. You will receive monthly distributions from cash flow, but principal is not accessible until the property is sold or refinanced. Only invest capital you can afford to have locked up for five years or longer.

**Q: Is there any way to sell or transfer my interest before the property is sold?**

A: The operating agreement permits transfers subject to conditions:

- **GP Consent Required** -- Prior written consent, which may be withheld at GP discretion
- **Securities Law Compliance** -- Must comply with SEC Regulation D and applicable laws
- **Right of First Refusal** -- The GP may have a right of first refusal
- **Transfer Documentation** -- Buyer must execute all required partnership documents

Gray Capital may assist in finding a buyer but this is not guaranteed. Transfers typically occur at a discount to NAV due to illiquidity. Plan to hold for the full term.

**Q: What is Gray Capital's primary exit strategy for Fairmont Apartments?**

A: The primary exit is a sale of the property in Year 7 (projected June 2033) after the business plan is fully executed -- loss-to-lease captured, concessions burned off, NOI growth compounded, and ~8 years of tax abatement still remaining for the next buyer. The exit will be marketed through a leading commercial brokerage (CBRE, JLL, Walker & Dunlop) to institutional buyers, regional operators, and other qualified purchasers. Gray Capital underwrites a 5.50% exit cap rate -- conservative relative to recent Columbus Class A comparables that have traded at 4.95%–5.10%.

**Q: Are there alternative exit strategies if market conditions are unfavorable?**

A: Yes. Gray Capital evaluates multiple exit paths:

1. **Refinance and Hold** -- If cap rates are elevated but NOI is growing, refinance to return investor capital while retaining the asset for future upside.
2. **Extended Hold** -- If the sale market is weak, extend the hold while the property continues generating cash flow and monthly distributions. Gray Capital will not sell into a down market just to meet a projected timeline.
3. **Partial Disposition** -- Sell portions of a portfolio or adjacent parcels separately while retaining the core asset.
4. **1031 Exchange** -- Roll proceeds into a qualifying replacement property to defer capital gains and depreciation recapture taxes.

**Q: What factors determine when Gray Capital decides to sell?**

A: Key factors include:

- **Business plan completion** -- Is loss-to-lease captured, concessions burned off, rents at target, and occupancy stabilized?
- **Market conditions** -- Are cap rates, buyer demand, and financing conditions favorable?
- **Return optimization** -- Does selling now maximize IRR and equity multiple? Sometimes holding longer increases returns; other times selling early boosts IRR.
- **Debt maturity** -- The fixed-rate loan at 5.5% has a maturity date that creates a natural decision point.
- **Macroeconomic environment** -- Interest rates, inflation, and broader conditions affect buyer demand and financing availability.

The GP retains final exit decision authority per the operating agreement but considers investor feedback and communicates transparently via quarterly reports.

**Q: What happens to my investment at the time of sale?**

A: Sale proceeds are distributed through the waterfall:

1. **Debt Repayment** -- Outstanding mortgage and secured obligations repaid
2. **Closing Costs** -- Brokerage commissions, transfer taxes, legal fees deducted
3. **Return of Capital** -- Investors receive remaining invested capital
4. **Accrued Preferred Return** -- Any unpaid 8% cumulative preferred return paid to LPs
5. **Profit Split** -- Remaining net proceeds split 70% LP / 30% GP

Final distributions are typically wired within 30-60 days after the sale closes, accompanied by a complete accounting statement.

**Q: What if the market is unfavorable when it is time to sell?**

A: Gray Capital is not forced to liquidate at a predetermined date. If the market is weak, the firm can extend the hold, refinance to return capital, or pursue alternatives. The property continues generating cash flow during any extension. Gray Capital stress-tests under multiple exit scenarios, including the Conservative scenario modeling a less favorable exit environment.

**Q: Will I receive updates on the exit timeline?**

A: Yes. Gray Capital provides updates through monthly operational updates and quarterly financial reports. As the property approaches the exit window, a commercial broker will conduct a formal broker opinion of value (BOV). Investors receive specific communications about the exit process and projected proceeds well in advance. **Q: If Gray Capital refinances instead of selling, how does that affect me?**

A: A cash-out refinance lets Gray Capital place new (larger) debt based on increased property value, distributing excess proceeds to investors. You receive a return of some or all of your original capital while retaining your ownership interest -- continuing to receive monthly distributions and participate in future upside at sale. Refinance proceeds are generally treated as return of capital (not taxable income) until they exceed your tax basis. Consult your CPA for specifics.
