# FAQ: Returns, Distributions & Cash Flow

**Q: What is the target return for the Fairmont Apartments investment?**

A: Base case projections over a 7-year hold: 14–16% IRR, 2.2x equity multiple, and 8.4% average annual cash-on-cash return. These are based on Gray Capital's base scenario assumptions and are not guaranteed. Conservative, Base, and Upside scenarios are provided so investors can evaluate the range of outcomes. The deal also targets an 8% cumulative preferred return paid before GP profit participation. Projections are not guarantees of future performance. Past performance is not indicative of future results.

**Q: What is Gray Capital's historical return performance?**

A: Across 10 realized deals, Gray Capital has delivered an average IRR of 29.3% and average equity multiple of 2.16x, with zero capital losses. Past performance is not indicative of future results.

**Q: How often are distributions paid?**

A: Monthly via direct deposit (ACH), typically within 30 days after month-end. A distribution statement accompanies each payment and is available in the investor portal.

**Q: When should I expect my first distribution?**

A: Typically within 60-90 days after property closing. There is an initial ramp-up period as the property transitions to Gray Capital management. After the first distribution, payments are monthly.

**Q: What is the preferred return and how does it work?**

A: The preferred return is 8% cumulative annually. LPs receive the first 8% of returns before the GP participates in any profit sharing above return of capital. The pref accrues from your capital contribution date and is cumulative -- if cash flow is insufficient in any period, the shortfall carries forward and must be made up before the GP earns any promote.

**Q: How does the distribution waterfall work step by step?**

A: The waterfall governs how all profits (cash flow and sale proceeds) are allocated:

1. **Return of Capital** -- 100% to investors until original capital is returned in full.
2. **Preferred Return** -- Investors receive 8% cumulative annualized preferred return. Any unpaid pref from prior periods is paid before the next tier.
3. **No GP Catch-Up** -- No catch-up provision. The GP does not receive a disproportionate share to "catch up" to a target split.
4. **Profit Split** -- Remaining profits split 70% LP / 30% GP.

This applies to both ongoing distributions and final sale proceeds.

**Q: What does "no GP catch-up" mean and why does it matter?**

A: A catch-up lets the GP receive 100% of profits after the pref is met until the GP reaches a target percentage, reducing what LPs receive in that tranche. Gray Capital does not use a catch-up -- after the 8% pref, profits go directly to the 80/20 split. Investors receive a larger share of profits, making this a more investor-friendly structure.

**Q: Are distributions guaranteed?**

A: No. Distributions depend on property operating performance -- occupancy, rental income, expenses, debt service, capex, and reserves. Gray Capital may retain cash for renovations, seasonal fluctuations, or economic buffers. The preferred return accrues if not paid, but payment is contingent on available cash flow.

**Q: What happens if the property cannot pay the full preferred return in a given period?**

A: The unpaid amount accrues and carries forward. Accrued preferred return must be paid in full before the GP receives any promote. Gray Capital builds operating reserves to help smooth distributions, but there is no guarantee the pref will be paid every period.

**Q: What is the difference between IRR, cash-on-cash return, and equity multiple?**

A: Three distinct performance measures:

- **IRR (Internal Rate of Return)** -- Annualized return accounting for timing and magnitude of all cash flows. Rewards faster capital return. Fairmont Apartments base case: 14-16% target IRR.
- **Cash-on-Cash (CoC) Return** -- Annual distributions as a percentage of original investment. A $100K investment receiving $7,200/year = 7.2% CoC. Measures ongoing cash flow yield only.
- **Equity Multiple** -- Total cash returned divided by total cash invested. A 2.2x multiple means $2.20 back for every $1.00 invested ($100K investment returns $220K total, or $120K profit).

All three matter: IRR rewards speed, equity multiple shows total wealth creation, CoC shows annual income.

**Q: Can I reinvest my distributions?**

A: Gray Capital does not offer a formal DRIP. However, you can accumulate distributions and invest them into future offerings. Many existing LPs reinvest into subsequent deals to compound returns.

**Q: What happens to my investment at the time of sale?**

A: Sale proceeds flow through the waterfall: mortgage and secured obligations repaid, closing costs deducted, then remaining proceeds distributed to investors -- return of capital, accrued preferred return, and finally the 80/20 LP/GP profit split on remaining gains. Investors receive a final distribution statement with complete accounting.
