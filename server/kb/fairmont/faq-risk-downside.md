# FAQ — Risk & Downside Scenarios (Fairmont at Westerville Commons)

---

### What are the biggest risks to this investment?
See the "Risk Factors" section for a summary. Illiquidity, local economic conditions, loss of tax abatement, and execution risk are the most material.

---

### What if the market turns downward after closing?
- The base case is modeled with conservative assumptions (lower rent growth, higher exit cap rate than current market). In a downturn, distributions could be reduced or suspended, and exit proceeds could be materially lower.

---

### What happens if the property does not lease up as projected?
- Lower occupancy reduces NOI and may violate debt covenants. Mitigations include experienced management (Gray Residential), operating reserves, and the option to adjust renovation timing.

---

### What if interest rates rise or refinancing is not available at exit?
- The 10-year agency debt is fixed, with 48 months of IO; no refinancing is required during hold. However, if cap rates rise, exit pricing could be materially lower. In a worst-case scenario, capital loss is possible. See Monte Carlo scenario in Risks.

---

### Could the tax abatement be lost, and what would that mean?
- If the abatement is removed due to non-compliance or policy change, annual RE taxes would rise and cash flow/returns would fall sharply. The property has positive value even without abatement, but projected returns drop substantially (see downside case in Tax Abatement KB).

---

### Has Gray Capital ever lost investor principal?
No principal losses on any prior syndicated Gray Capital offering to date. Past results do not guarantee future performance.

---

*This summary is not exhaustive; refer to the PPM for a full risk discussion.*
